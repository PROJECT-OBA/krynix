# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and maintainers pledge to make participation in the Krynix project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, and technically rigorous community.

## Our Standards

**Expected behavior:**

- Providing and accepting constructive technical feedback
- Focusing on what is best for the project and its users
- Showing respect for differing viewpoints and experiences
- Acknowledging mistakes and learning from them
- Prioritizing correctness, security, and reliability in all contributions

**Unacceptable behavior:**

- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- Sustained disruption of project discussion or development
- Any conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces, including the repository, issue tracker, pull requests, discussions, and any other communication channel used for project business. It also applies when an individual is officially representing the project in public spaces.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the project maintainers at **conduct@krynix.dev**.

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. Maintainers are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the reporter of any incident.

### Enforcement Actions

1. **Warning** — A private written warning with clarity on the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate.
2. **Temporary Ban** — A temporary ban from any form of interaction or public communication with the project.
3. **Permanent Ban** — A permanent ban from any form of interaction or public communication with the project.
